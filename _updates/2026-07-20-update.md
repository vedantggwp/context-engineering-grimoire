# Update Report — Context Engineering for LLM Agents

Date: 2026-07-20 | Mode: pr
Since: 2026-07-12 | Policy: min score 12, max 5 sources (policy source: file)

## What's New

The delta scout's temporal angles ("what's new since 2026-07-12") returned almost
entirely secondary commentary — aggregators and SEO restatements of sources the wiki
already had. The angles that paid off were **corpus-gap** angles, and they surfaced
three genuine holes: the wiki had no coverage of agent memory at all, its "isolate"
strategy was described only abstractly through LangChain's taxonomy, and its only
long-context degradation source was Liu et al. (2023).

Four P0 sources were ingested to close those gaps. Chroma's **Context Rot** is the
length-isolating successor to lost-in-the-middle — 18 frontier models, task complexity
held constant, 194,480 LLM calls — and it includes a genuinely counterintuitive result:
models perform *better* on shuffled haystacks than logically coherent ones. Anthropic's
**multi-agent research system** post is the production account of context isolation,
including its costs (~15× tokens) and, unusually for a vendor post, an explicit statement
of where the pattern does not apply. The **agent memory survey** (47 authors) and
**Context Engineering 2.0** are both consolidation attempts on fragmented vocabulary.

The structural effect is larger than the article count suggests: the wiki's content graph
went from **3 connected components to 1**. Memory and multi-agent isolation were the
missing bridges between the strategy cluster and everything else.

Two of the four new articles rest on arXiv **abstracts alone** and say so at the top, in a
warning box, before any content. They are maps to their sources, not summaries of them.
This is the honest ceiling of the `/abs` fetch route and is now recorded as a known
limitation in `approved-sources.md`.
